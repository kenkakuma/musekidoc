## Entries Created

### 1. Shino (志野焼)
- **Region**: 岐阜县·美濃地区
- **Type**: 炻器 (Stoneware)
- **Period**: 16th century (Azuchi-Momoyama period)
- **Key Features**:
  - Thick milky white feldspar glaze
  - Red/gray natural speckles
  - Japan's only original white pottery
  - Low temperature, long firing with slow cooling
- **Related Artists**: kuwata-takuro
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative English sources

### 2. Oribe (織部焼)
- **Region**: 岐阜县·美濃地区
- **Type**: 炻器 (Stoneware)
- **Period**: Late 16th - early 17th century (1596-1624)
- **Key Features**:
  - Distinctive green copper glaze
  - Bold geometric patterns
  - First use of colored stoneware glaze in Japan
  - Asymmetric, eccentric shapes
  - Named after tea master Furuta Oribe
- **Related Artists**: None in current database
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 3. Ki-Seto / Koiseto (黄瀬戸)
- **Region**: 爱知县·濑户地区 / 岐阜县·美濃地区
- **Type**: 陶器 (Pottery)
- **Period**: Late Muromachi period (1338-1573) onwards
- **Key Features**:
  - Warm yellow glaze from iron-ash glaze
  - Carved floral designs
  - Green or iron-brown accents
  - Tampan decoration technique
- **Related Artists**: takeuchi-shingo
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 4. Karatsu (唐津焼)
- **Region**: 佐贺县·唐津地区（九州）
- **Type**: 炻器 (Stoneware)
- **Period**: Mid-16th century
- **Key Features**:
  - First glazed ware in Japan
  - Korean origin and influence
  - Kick-wheel and climbing kiln technology
  - Simple, rustic beauty (wabi-sabi)
  - "First Raku, second Hagi, third Karatsu"
- **Related Artists**: shimomura-atsushi
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 5. Hagi (萩焼)
- **Region**: 山口县·萩市
- **Type**: 陶器 (Pottery)
- **Period**: Late 16th century (circa 1604)
- **Key Features**:
  - Korean potters origin (Ri brothers)
  - Earthy texture from local Kuchi-tsuchi clay
  - Kan-nyuu (貫入) crazing
  - Nanabake (七化) - seven transformations
  - "First Raku, second Hagi, third Karatsu"
- **Related Artists**: None in current database
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 6. Raku (楽焼)
- **Region**: 京都府
- **Type**: 陶器 (Pottery)
- **Period**: Late 1570s
- **Key Features**:
  - Hand-formed (tezukune) technique
  - No potter's wheel
  - Low-temperature firing
  - Lead glazes
  - Commissioned by Sen no Rikyu
  - 16 generations of Raku family
  - "First Raku, second Hagi, third Karatsu"
- **Related Artists**: None in current database
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 7. Mashiko (益子焼)
- **Region**: 栃木县·益子町
- **Type**: 陶器 (Pottery)
- **Period**: Elevated to art in 1924
- **Key Features**:
  - Folk pottery (mingei)
  - Hamada Shoji's influence
  - Earthy textures, natural glazes
  - Rustic feel
  - 300+ potters working today
  - Hand-made, utilitarian, affordable
- **Related Artists**: hamada-tomoo, ishikawa-wakahiko, yoshizawa-hiro
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 8. Kutani (九谷焼)
- **Region**: 石川县·加贺地区
- **Type**: 瓷器 (Porcelain)
- **Period**: From 1655
- **Key Features**:
  - Overglaze enamel decoration
  - Kutani Gosai (five colors): green, yellow, red, purple, blue
  - Ko-Kutani (Old Kutani) and Saiko-Kutani (Revived Kutani) periods
  - Vivid, translucent colors
  - Living National Treasure: Tokuda Yasokichi III
- **Related Artists**: None in current database
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 9. Imari-Arita (伊万里焼·有田焼)
- **Region**: 佐贺县·有田地区
- **Type**: 瓷器 (Porcelain)
- **Period**: From 1616
- **Key Features**:
  - First porcelain production in Japan
  - Korean potter Ri Sampei (Yi Sam-pyong)
  - Discovery of kaolin
  - Blue and white (sometsuke)
  - Overglaze enamels
  - Exported extensively to Europe
- **Related Artists**: None in current database
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 10. Kakiemon (柿右衛門)
- **Region**: 佐贺县·有田地区
- **Type**: 瓷器 (Porcelain)
- **Period**: Mid-17th century
- **Key Features**:
  - Milky white nigoshide glaze
  - Delicate overglaze enamels
  - Sparse decoration emphasizing white porcelain
  - Iron red, light blue, bluish-green, yellow colors
  - Founded by Sakaida Kakiemon (1596-1666)
  - Imitated by European manufacturers
  - Important Intangible Cultural Property (1971)
- **Related Artists**: None in current database
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 11. Kyo-yaki / Kiyomizu-yaki (京焼·清水焼)
- **Region**: 京都府
- **Type**: 陶器·瓷器 (Pottery & Porcelain)
- **Period**: From 17th century
- **Key Features**:
  - Diversity of styles (no single characteristic)
  - Nishiki-de technique by Nonomura Ninsei
  - Okuda Eisen's porcelain innovation
  - Aoki Mokubei's true porcelain
  - 400 years history, 300+ kilns
  - Finest materials and advanced techniques
- **Related Artists**: None in current database
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 12. Iga (伊賀焼)
- **Region**: 三重县·伊贺市
- **Type**: 炻器 (Stoneware)
- **Period**: Ancient (parallel with Shigaraki)
- **Key Features**:
  - Ancient Lake Biwa clay (3-4 million years old)
  - High heat resistance
  - Reddened surface
  - Glassy green vidro glaze
  - Distinctive "ears" (mimitsuki) on tea vessels
  - "Iga has ears, Shigaraki has none"
- **Related Artists**: endo-takashi
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

### 13. Kasama (笠間焼)
- **Region**: 茨城县·笠间市
- **Type**: 陶器 (Pottery)
- **Period**: 1772-1781 (Edo period) to present
- **Key Features**:
  - "Free pottery" philosophy
  - No fixed style or format
  - Emphasizes artist individuality
  - 300+ potters using various clays and glazes
  - Post-WWII transformation from utilitarian to artistic
  - Ibaraki Pottery Training Centre (1950)
- **Related Artists**: None in current database
- **Sources**: 3 authoritative sources

## Notable Insights

### "Three Great Tea Wares" Documented
All three wares from the famous saying "一乐二萩三唐津" (First Raku, second Hagi, third Karatsu) are now documented with comprehensive entries.

### Korean Influence
Multiple entries (Karatsu, Hagi, Imari-Arita, Kakiemon) document the crucial role of Korean potters in Japanese ceramics development, particularly:
- Technology transfer (kick-wheel, climbing kiln)
- Porcelain production techniques
- Glazing innovations

### Mingei Movement
Mashiko entry documents the folk pottery (mingei) movement and Hamada Shoji's crucial role in elevating folk pottery to art.

### Western Export & Influence
- Imari-Arita: Extensive exports to Europe via Dutch East India Company
- Kakiemon: Widely imitated by European porcelain manufacturers
- Kutani: Revered for overglaze enamel techniques

### Living Traditions
- Raku: 16 generations of Raku family continuing
- Mashiko: 300+ potters working today
- Kasama: 300+ potters in "free pottery" style
- Kyo-Kiyomizu: 300+ kilns in Kyoto
